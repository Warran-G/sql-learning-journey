# Customer Churn Analysis

## Dataset Overview
 Total customers: 7,043
 Total columns: 21

## Insight 1: Overall Churn Rate

 Customers retained: 5,174 (73.46%)
 Customers churned: 1,869 (26.54%)

### Business Interpretation
Approximately 1 in 4 customers leave the company. Reducing churn could have a significant impact on revenue and customer lifetime value.

 ## Insight 2: Contract Type and Churn

### Key Finding
Month-to-month customers have a significantly higher churn rate (42.71%) compared to one-year (11.27%) and two-year contracts (2.83%).

### Business Interpretation
There is a strong inverse relationship between contract duration and churn. Customers with longer commitments are significantly more likely to stay.

### Recommendation
Focus retention strategies on converting month-to-month customers into longer-term contracts through incentives or pricing models.

## Insight 3: Payment Method and Churn

### Key Finding
Customers using electronic check have the highest churn rate (45.29%), while automatic payment methods such as credit card (15.24%) and bank transfer (16.71%) show significantly lower churn.

### Interpretation
Manual payment methods are associated with higher churn risk, possibly due to lower customer commitment or convenience barriers.

### Recommendation
Encourage customers to switch to automatic payment methods through incentives, discounts, or simplified onboarding.

## Insight 4: Customer Tenure and Churn

### Key Finding
Customers with low tenure (1–5 months) show extremely high churn rates (up to 61.99%), while long-term customers (60+ months) have very low churn rates (below 10%).

### Interpretation
Churn is heavily concentrated in the early stages of the customer lifecycle, indicating potential onboarding or initial service experience issues.

### Recommendation
Focus retention strategies on the first 90 days of the customer journey, including onboarding improvements, early engagement, and targeted support.
